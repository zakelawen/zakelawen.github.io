About

I am Jinshuo Zhang, an MSc student at Qilu University of Technology (Shandong Academy of Sciences). My research centers on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Mechanistic Interpretability (MI).

On the RAG side, I focus on two core questions: when a large language model should retrieve external knowledge, and how it can make more reliable use of the context it retrieves.

I am equally drawn to mechanistic interpretability. Today's large language models are remarkably capable, yet their inner workings remain largely a black box. I want to understand what a model is really "thinking" and why it produces a particular output — because I believe that only by truly reading the internal mechanisms of these models can we build AI systems that are safer, more reliable, and more trustworthy.

Findings of ACL 2026 CCF-A ● Accepted

D²-RAG: Dual-Decision Retrieval-Augmented Generation via Multi-Dimensional Uncertainty and Utility-Aware Decoding

Jinshuo Zhang, Xiaoding Zhou, Weiyu Zhang*, Guoqiang Chen, Ying Lian, Xiaoyang Meng, Yonghe Chen, Hongjiao Guan, Jiasheng Si, Wenpeng Lu

Addressing two key questions in RAG — when to retrieve and how to use retrieved results — we propose a Dual-Decision RAG framework. It fuses uncertainty signals from probability distributions, internal states, output consistency, and self-checking confidence to decide whether a query needs external knowledge. A utility-aware contrastive decoding strategy then adaptively modulates the decoding process according to the quality of the retrieved context, improving the use of reliable information while suppressing retrieval noise.

EMNLP 2026 CCF-B ○ Under Review

Steering LLM Representations for Retrieved-Context Utility Assessment

Jinshuo Zhang, Xiaoding Zhou, Yan Teng, Weitian Zhang, Guoqiang Chen, Ying Lian, Jiasheng Si, Weiyu Zhang*, Wenpeng Lu

Semantic relevance often fails to reflect whether a retrieved document actually helps answer a question. We propose a representation-based method for assessing retrieved-context utility: by injecting a trainable Context Separator Vector into a frozen LLM, we sharpen the representational separability between answer-supporting and distracting documents, and predict document utility with a prototype classifier. The resulting utility score can further drive context gating at generation time, reducing the interference of low-quality retrievals.

CSCWD 2026 CCF-C ● Accepted

CKG-RAG: Enhancing LLM Reasoning in Medical Domain Using Contextual Knowledge Graphs

Jinshuo Zhang, Xiaoding Zhou, Xinxiao Qiao, Weiyu Zhang*, Guoqiang Chen, Xiaoli Zheng, Cong Liu, Wenpeng Lu

In medical question answering, conventional RAG tends to introduce redundant context and cause the "lost-in-the-middle" problem in LLMs. We propose a retrieval-augmented generation framework built on contextual knowledge graphs: reliable external knowledge is retrieved from multiple medical sources, and an LLM extracts entities and relations to construct a question-specific contextual knowledge graph. A GNN-MLP mapping module then encodes the graph into Graph Tokens, injected as a soft prompt to strengthen the model's structured reasoning.
